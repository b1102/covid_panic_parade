Covid panic parade: a project that compares the amount of deaths with fraction of Covid news between different countries. It does it using 2 parameters:
• fraction of Covid news
• deaths in the last 7 days/1M pop

Then both parameters are used to form two lists/charts with different sorting. So for every country we have two numbers:
• a position of countries in a list, sorted by a fraction of Covid news as part of all news, let's call it Pnews
• a position of countries in a list, sorted by number of deaths from Covid per 1m people in last 7 days, let's call it Pdeaths

The assumption is that in an ideal world these two should be around the same for a country, which means more people are dying from Covid, more news we could expect about it. But sometimes we could have anomalies:
•
Panic detected
- too much news, compared to the amount of deaths. Criteria: Pnews - Pdeaths ≥ 6
•
Threat underestimated
- many deaths, but not much news about it. Criteria: Pdeaths - Pnews ≥ 6

Both cases are kind of data anomalies and are bad situations, however from different sides.
